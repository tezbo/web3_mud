## Textiles & Fabrics

### Sunward Kingdoms
- **Wool**: Common, warm, used for cloaks and peasant clothing
- **Linen**: Practical, cool, used for undergarments and summer wear
- **Leather**: Cow, goat, sheep - for armor, boots, belts
- **Fur**: Bear, wolf, fox - for winter cloaks and trim
- **Canvas**: Heavy cloth for tents, sails, work clothes

### Twilight Dominion
- **Silk**: Prized fabric, multiple qualities from common to imperial
- **Brocade**: Patterned silk, used for formal wear
- **Cotton**: Common daily wear, dyed in vibrant colors
- **Rice paper**: For screens, lanterns, temporary walls
- **Lacquered fabric**: Treated cloth that resists water

### Shadowfen
- **Mire-leather**: Tanned from swamp creatures, water-resistant
- **Patchwork cloth**: Salvaged scraps sewn together
- **Reed-weave**: Woven from swamp reeds, rough but functional
- **Salvaged silk**: Twilight silk that made it through the Scar
- **Oiled canvas**: Waterproofed cloth for survival gear

## Wood & Timber

### General (All Realms)
- **Oak**: Strong, common, used for construction and furniture
- **Pine**: Softer, cheaper, used for temporary structures
- **Ash**: Flexible, used for weapon hafts and tool handles
- **Maple**: Fine grain, used for crafting and instruments
- **Willow**: Flexible, used for baskets and bows

### Special Woods
- **Ironwood** (Rare): Extremely dense, sinks in water, used for clubs and mallets
- **Silverwood** (Sunward): Silver-barked trees, wood has faint metallic sheen
- **Jade Bamboo** (Twilight): Not true bamboo, but similar - used in construction
- **Shadowfen Cypress**: Dark, twisted wood from the swamps, naturally preserved
- **Scar-touched wood**: Wood petrified by Scar energies, unnaturally hard

---

## Stone & Minerals

### Building Materials
- **Granite**: Sunward's primary building stone - gray, durable
- **Limestone**: Softer, carved for decorative elements
- **Slate**: Roofing material, splits into flat sheets
- **Marble**: Rare, used for important buildings and statues
- **Jade**: Twilight's signature stone - green, hard, polished to high sheen
- **Obsidian**: Volcanic glass, used for cutting tools and decorative items

### Gem stones (All Rare)
- **Emerald**: Green, valuable
- **Ruby**: Red, valuable
- **Sapphire**: Blue, valuable
- **Amethyst**: Purple, moderately valuable
- **Citrine**: Yellow, common semi-precious
- **Thread-crystal**: Rare gems that glow when near active threadsinging

---

## Pre-Sundering Materials (Lost Technology)

These are legendary materials that can't be created anymore:

- **Eternal Steel**: Pre-Sundering metal that never dulls or rusts
- **Woven Glass**: Flexible glass fabric created by master Singers
- **True-thread Cloth**: Fabric that shifts color based on wearer's mood
- **Lightstone**: Stone that glows without heat, powered by ancient singing
- **Phase-wood**: Wood that can be temporarily softened by singing

**Lore Note**: These are valuable quest rewards or ancient artifacts. NPCs might mention them wistfully.

## Food & Consumables

### Common Foods (Realm-Agnostic)
- Bread, cheese, ale, wine, stew, dried meat, apples, root vegetables

### Sunward Specialties
- Honey-wheat bread, roast boar, ale (dark and strong), mutton

### Twilight Specialties
- Rice dishes, steamed fish, tea (ceremonial), pickled vegetables, sake

### Shadowfen Specialties
- Eel stew, swamp greens, hard liquor, smoked fish, preserved rations

## FORBIDDEN Materials (Do Not Use)

These break immersion and should NEVER appear:

❌ Plastic, vinyl, synthetic fabrics  
❌ Modern alloys (aluminum, titanium, stainless steel)  
❌ Gunpowder, explosives, firearms  
❌ Electricity, batteries, electronics  
❌ Rubber (natural or synthetic)  
❌ Glass fiber, carbon fiber  
❌ Modern dyes (neon colors, synthetic pigments)

**Wordsmith**: Reference these materials in descriptions  
**Personality Designer**: NPCs mention materials relevant to their trade  
**Quest Architect**: Quests can involve finding rare materials  
**Mapmaker**: Consider what buildings are made of in each realm
