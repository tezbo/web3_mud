### Room List with Exits

1. **Market Square**
   - Exits: North (Armorer's Shop), East (Apothecary's Hut), South (Shadowfen Inn), West (Secret Alley), Down (Smuggler's Tunnel)
   - Purpose: The bustling heart of the district, where traders and adventurers converge. It acts as a central hub with multiple exits leading to specialized shops and hidden paths.

2. **Armorer's Shop**
   - Exits: East (Market Square), South (Apothecary's Hut)
   - Purpose: Offers weapons and armor crafted from local materials. Positioned for easy access from the market, encouraging players to upgrade gear.

3. **Apothecary's Hut**
   - Exits: North (Market Square), East (Shadowfen Inn)
   - Purpose: Provides potions and remedies, crucial for survival in the swamp. Located near the inn for travelers needing rest and recuperation.

4. **Shadowfen Inn**
   - Exits: North (Market Square), Up (Guest Rooms), Down (Cellar)
   - Purpose: A place of rest with multiple floors. The inn's cellar might hint at more than just storage, creating intrigue.

5. **Secret Alley**
   - Exits: West (Market Square)
   - Purpose: Hidden path for players who explore. Could lead to small quests or encounters, adding depth to the district.

6. **Smuggler's Tunnel**
   - Exits: Up (Market Square)
   - Purpose: A hidden area accessed from the Market Square, offering a shortcut or secretive escape route. Rewards exploration with potential treasure or unique encounters.

### Design Notes

- **Flow and Purpose**: The Market Square serves as the nexus, with major paths leading outward, ensuring that players naturally encounter key locations. Merchant services are clustered around the square for convenience.
  
- **Exploration and Discovery**: The Secret Alley and Smuggler's Tunnel provide hidden paths that reward curious players. These secondary paths enhance the storytelling by suggesting illicit activities within the district.

- **Vertical Space**: The Shadowfen Inn uses vertical complexity with guest rooms above and a mysterious cellar below, encouraging players to explore different levels.

- **Realism**: The layout mimics a real-world market district, with shops directly accessible from the central square and supportive facilities nearby. Hidden areas are logically placed to represent the underbelly of trade activities.

This design balances accessibility with intrigue, encouraging both quick visits and deeper exploration.
