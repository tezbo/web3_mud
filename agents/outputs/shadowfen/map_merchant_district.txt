### Room List with Exits

1. **Market Square**
   - **Exits**: North to Armorer, East to Alchemist Shop, South to Pawnshop
   - **Purpose**: Central hub of the district; a bustling area with vendors and traders. It encourages exploration by leading to specialized shops.

2. **Armorer**
   - **Exits**: South to Market Square, East to Tailor
   - **Purpose**: Sells armor and protective gear. Positioned to be easily accessible from the market for adventurers looking to gear up.

3. **Tailor**
   - **Exits**: West to Armorer, South to Alchemist Shop
   - **Purpose**: Provides clothing and light armor. Its proximity to the Armorer fosters a logical flow for players upgrading their gear.

4. **Alchemist Shop**
   - **Exits**: West to Market Square, North to Tailor, South to Warehouse
   - **Purpose**: Offers potions and arcane supplies. Placed strategically between the Market and Tailor to attract a variety of foot traffic.

5. **Pawnshop**
   - **Exits**: North to Market Square, East to Warehouse
   - **Purpose**: Buys and sells miscellaneous items. It's a place for players to offload loot or find unique items. Its location near the Market makes for convenient trading.

6. **Warehouse**
   - **Exits**: North to Alchemist Shop, West to Pawnshop
   - **Purpose**: Storage for goods and potential quest-related activities. Positioned at the district's edge, it serves as a logical end-point for deliveries and smuggling operations.

### Design Notes

- **Flow**: The Market Square serves as the central hub, ensuring players naturally pass through it to reach other locations. This design encourages players to explore each shop, creating a sense of bustling activity and commerce.
- **Discovery**: The district provides a straightforward exploration path with interconnected shops. Each shop leads logically to the next, simulating the experience of wandering through a real market district.
- **Pacing**: The spatial arrangement allows for a balance between exploration and convenience, with short distances between key locations, while maintaining a sense of district scale.
- **Realism**: The layout mirrors a traditional urban market setup, with related businesses clustered together, making the district feel both functional and lively.
