### Merchant District in Shadowfen Style

```
       [Market Square]
           /     \
     [Armory]   [Potion Shop]
        |           |
 [Blacksmith]     [Herbalist]
        |
    [Tannery]
```

#### Room List with Exits and Design Notes:

1. **Market Square**
   - **Exits:** North to Armory, East to Potion Shop
   - **Purpose:** Central hub and gathering point. Bustling with vendors and merchants, it's the heart of the district. Sets the tone with an open-air market feel, ideal for player interactions and quest givers.
   - **Design Notes:** As the central hub, this space encourages exploration by offering multiple exits to specialized shops.

2. **Armory**
   - **Exits:** South to Market Square, South to Blacksmith
   - **Purpose:** Sells armor and defensive gear. Players come here to prepare for battles.
   - **Design Notes:** Positioned near the Market Square for easy access, with a direct path to the Blacksmith for crafting needs.

3. **Potion Shop**
   - **Exits:** West to Market Square, South to Herbalist
   - **Purpose:** Specializes in potions and alchemical supplies. Offers quests related to rare ingredients.
   - **Design Notes:** Flow from Market Square to Potion Shop encourages players to explore further, leading naturally to the Herbalist for ingredient gathering.

4. **Blacksmith**
   - **Exits:** North to Armory, South to Tannery
   - **Purpose:** Offers weapon crafting and repair services. Essential for players focusing on combat.
   - **Design Notes:** Logical connection from Armory to Blacksmith, allowing players to seamlessly transition from buying armor to upgrading weapons.

5. **Herbalist**
   - **Exits:** North to Potion Shop
   - **Purpose:** Sells herbs and natural remedies. Engages players in gathering quests and trades with the Potion Shop.
   - **Design Notes:** Positioned as a natural extension of the Potion Shop, reinforcing a supply chain narrative.

6. **Tannery**
   - **Exits:** North to Blacksmith
   - **Purpose:** Processes hides into leather goods. Provides materials for crafting and bartering.
   - **Design Notes:** The southernmost point of the district, offering a sense of progression from metalwork (Blacksmith) to leatherwork, completing the crafting loop.

### Design Overview
- **Flow:** The central Market Square encourages initial exploration, with specialized shops radiating outwards. Each shop is positioned to logically support nearby businesses, creating a natural flow of commerce.
- **Discovery:** While the district is compact, the variety of shops offers opportunities for players to uncover quests and trades that require visiting multiple locations.
- **Realism:** The spatial arrangement reflects a realistic merchant district where trade and crafting are interconnected, providing a believable and immersive environment.
