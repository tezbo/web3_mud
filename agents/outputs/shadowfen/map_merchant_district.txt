### Merchant District in Shadowfen Style

```
          [Alchemist's Shop]
                 |
                 N
                 |
[Marketplace] - W + E - [Bank]
                 |
                 S
                 |
 [Blacksmith] - W + E - [Tailor's Shop]
                 |
                 S
                 |
             [Docks]
```

### Room List with Exits

1. **Marketplace**
   - Exits: North (N) to Alchemist's Shop, East (E) to Bank, South (S) to Blacksmith
   - Purpose: Central hub for the district where players start their exploration. It's a bustling area filled with stalls and NPC vendors. 

2. **Alchemist's Shop**
   - Exits: South (S) to Marketplace
   - Purpose: A specialized shop selling potions and magical reagents. It's slightly isolated to enhance the mysterious feel of the shop.

3. **Bank**
   - Exits: West (W) to Marketplace
   - Purpose: Secure storage for player valuables. Positioned to be easily accessible from the Marketplace for convenience.

4. **Blacksmith**
   - Exits: North (N) to Marketplace, East (E) to Tailor's Shop
   - Purpose: Essential for players to upgrade and repair their equipment. Close enough to the Marketplace for ease of access, with a side street leading to the Tailor.

5. **Tailor's Shop**
   - Exits: West (W) to Blacksmith, South (S) to Docks
   - Purpose: Offers clothing and armor customization. Positioned to create a natural flow between trades with the Blacksmith. 

6. **Docks**
   - Exits: North (N) to Tailor's Shop
   - Purpose: Farthest point from the Marketplace, serving as a transition to potential water-based adventures or quests. It provides a scenic exit from the district.

### Design Notes

- **Flow and Exploration**: The Marketplace serves as the central hub, naturally leading players to explore the essential services like the Blacksmith and Bank. The Alchemistâ€™s Shop is slightly off the main path to encourage discovery.

- **Convenience vs. Exploration**: Key services (Bank, Blacksmith) are close to the Marketplace for convenience, while more specialized services (Alchemist, Docks) require additional exploration.

- **Storytelling Through Space**: The placement of the Docks at the district's edge suggests potential for further adventures and trade with distant lands, enhancing the narrative of commerce and travel in Shadowfen.

- **Realism and Logical Organization**: The district layout reflects a logical organization of services, with related trades (Blacksmith and Tailor) positioned near each other, while the Marketplace acts as the commercial heart.

- **Layered Complexity**: Vertical space is not used here, focusing instead on horizontal exploration with potential for future underground or above-ground expansions (e.g., a cellar in the Alchemist's Shop or an upstairs office in the Bank).
