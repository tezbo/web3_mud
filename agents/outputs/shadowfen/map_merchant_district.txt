### Merchant District - Shadowfen Style

```
          [Blacksmith]
              |
          [Market Square] -- [General Store]
            /     |    \
      [Tavern]  [Alley]  [Guild Hall]
```

### Room List with Exits:

1. **Market Square**
   - **Exits**: North to Blacksmith, East to General Store, South to Alley, Southwest to Tavern, Southeast to Guild Hall
   - **Purpose/Design**: The bustling heart of the Merchant District, filled with stalls and traders. Acts as the central hub connecting to all key locations. Designed to encourage player interaction and trade.

2. **Blacksmith**
   - **Exits**: South to Market Square
   - **Purpose/Design**: A place for players to craft and repair weapons/armor. Positioned north of the Market Square to symbolize the upward journey towards strength and preparation.

3. **General Store**
   - **Exits**: West to Market Square
   - **Purpose/Design**: Supplies adventurers with general goods and necessities. Easy access from the Market Square facilitates quick restocking before heading out.

4. **Tavern**
   - **Exits**: Northeast to Market Square
   - **Purpose/Design**: A social hub for gathering information, recruiting companions, or just relaxing. Positioned to the southwest to suggest a retreat from the busy commerce of the square.

5. **Alley**
   - **Exits**: North to Market Square
   - **Purpose/Design**: A narrow passageway leading to a hidden area, encouraging exploration. Rumored to be the route to a secretive traders' guild or black market.

6. **Guild Hall**
   - **Exits**: Northwest to Market Square
   - **Purpose/Design**: The administrative center for merchants, where deals are made and disputes settled. Located southeast to imply a place of order and governance, away from the immediate hustle.

### Design Notes on Flow and Purpose:

- **Flow**: The Market Square serves as a central hub, allowing players to easily navigate to different areas based on their needs. The layout encourages movement through the square for both convenience and storytelling.
- **Discovery**: The Alley provides an element of mystery and exploration, rewarding players who stray from the beaten path with potential secrets.
- **Realism**: The arrangement of rooms mirrors a realistic town layout, with the Market Square as the focal point and related businesses logically positioned around it.
- **Pacing**: By clustering key locations around the Market Square and placing the Guild Hall slightly further, exploration feels both rewarding and appropriately paced.
- **Vertical Space**: The design is primarily horizontal to reflect the open, bustling nature of a market district, with vertical elements implied in future expansions (e.g., multi-story shops or hidden cellars).
