Welcome to the "Merchant District," a bustling trade hub in the heart of Shadowfen. This district is designed to offer a blend of commerce, exploration, and storytelling. The layout encourages players to explore diverse shops while maintaining a logical flow.

### Room List with Exits

1. **Market Square (Central Hub)**
   - Exits: North (Alchemist's Nook), East (Armorer's Forge), West (Jeweler's Den), South (Silk Emporium)
   - Purpose: A central hub bustling with activity. Merchants hawk their wares, and adventurers gather to trade stories and goods.

2. **Alchemist's Nook**
   - Exits: South (Market Square)
   - Purpose: A mysterious shop filled with potions and ingredients. The scent of herbs and chemicals fills the air, enticing curious adventurers.

3. **Jeweler's Den**
   - Exits: East (Market Square)
   - Purpose: A small, opulent shop where precious gems and artifacts are traded. The jeweler is known for crafting unique items for discerning customers.

4. **Armorer's Forge**
   - Exits: West (Market Square)
   - Purpose: The clanging of hammers echoes as skilled smiths forge weapons and armor. Adventurers frequent this shop to prepare for upcoming battles.

5. **Silk Emporium**
   - Exits: North (Market Square), South (Dockside Warehouse)
   - Purpose: A luxurious shop displaying exotic silks and fabrics. Traders from distant lands bring their finest wares for the discerning clientele.

6. **Dockside Warehouse**
   - Exits: North (Silk Emporium)
   - Purpose: A large storage area near the water's edge. Goods are unloaded from ships, creating a dynamic environment of commerce and intrigue.

### Design Notes

- **Flow and Purpose**: The Market Square serves as the central hub, ensuring all shops are within easy reach while maintaining a sense of distance and discovery. The layout encourages players to pass through the Market Square repeatedly, reinforcing its role as the district's focal point.

- **Exploration and Discovery**: Hidden interactions within shops, such as secret recipes in the Alchemist's Nook or rare gems in the Jeweler's Den, reward players who take time to explore.

- **Pacing and Realism**: The district's layout reflects a logical commercial area, with the Dockside Warehouse positioned near the water for realistic goods transport. The clustering of shops around the Market Square simulates real-world market dynamics.

- **Storytelling Through Space**: Each shop tells its own story through detailed descriptions and unique NPCs, providing a rich narrative layer to the district.

- **Vertical Space**: Although not explicitly designed in this layout, potential expansions could introduce vertical elements such as rooftop gardens or underground smuggling routes connected to the Dockside Warehouse.
