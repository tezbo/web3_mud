### Shadowfen Dungeon Map

```
         [Crypt]
            |
         [Tomb]
            |
        [Chamber]
       /    |    \
[Guardroom] | [Treasure]
            |
       [Entrance]
```

### Room List & Exits

1. **Entrance**
   - **Exits:** Up to Chamber
   - **Purpose:** Starting point for players entering the dungeon. Dimly lit with flickering torches, setting an ominous tone. Provides a sense of anticipation for the challenges ahead.

2. **Chamber**
   - **Exits:** Down to Entrance, Left to Guardroom, Right to Treasure, Up to Tomb
   - **Purpose:** Central hub of the dungeon. Acts as a junction with connections to several critical areas. Decorated with ancient runes hinting at the dungeon's history.

3. **Guardroom**
   - **Exits:** Right to Chamber
   - **Purpose:** Houses the remnants of ancient guards. Contains minor loot and serves as an early challenge. Offers an optional exploration path with modest rewards.

4. **Treasure**
   - **Exits:** Left to Chamber
   - **Purpose:** Hidden room requiring careful exploration to discover. Contains valuable loot and traps, rewarding players who explore thoroughly.

5. **Tomb**
   - **Exits:** Down to Chamber, Up to Crypt
   - **Purpose:** The resting place of an ancient entity. Contains clues and lore about the dungeon's origins. Acts as a transitional space to the final area.

6. **Crypt**
   - **Exits:** Down to Tomb
   - **Purpose:** The final room and the climax of the dungeon. Houses a powerful foe or puzzle. Offers significant rewards and story resolution for those who reach it.

### Design Notes

- **Flow:** Players start at the Entrance and naturally progress upward through the Chamber as a central hub, encouraging exploration of optional paths like the Guardroom and Treasure.
- **Exploration vs. Convenience:** The Chamber offers multiple paths, giving players choices and rewarding exploration. Hidden Treasure room encourages thorough investigation.
- **Storytelling:** The layout reflects an ancient burial site, with the Tomb and Crypt providing narrative depth through environmental storytelling.
- **Realism:** The vertical progression from Entrance to Crypt mimics descending deeper into an underground tomb.
- **Vertical Space:** Utilizes vertical space effectively with an upward progression, creating a sense of descending into the depths of an ancient crypt by moving upwards through the mapâ€™s structure.
