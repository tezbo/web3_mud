### Design Notes on Flow and Purpose

- **Central Hub (Central Hall):** The Central Hall serves as the dungeon's main intersection, ensuring that players naturally discover key areas while still offering the choice of direction. Its central location encourages exploration without overwhelming complexity.

- **Flow:** The dungeon balances linear progression with exploration. Players start at the Central Hall, with clear paths to combat (Guard's Quarters), resources (Armory), and secrets (Hidden Passage).

- **Discovery:** The Hidden Passage rewards players who stray from the main path. This passage also connects to the Treasure Chamber, creating a loop that encourages backtracking and further exploration.

- **Pacing:** The rooms are clustered to keep related activities (combat, exploration, resource gathering) close, while the hidden passage offers a sense of distance and accomplishment.

- **Realism:** The layout mimics a functional dungeon with living quarters, an armory, and a prison, making it believable and immersive.

This design leverages Shadowfen's mysterious and murky ambiance, providing players with a balance of combat, exploration, and narrative elements.
