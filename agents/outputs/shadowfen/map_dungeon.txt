### Room List with Exits

1. **Cave Entrance**
   - Exits: East to *Mossy Chamber*
   - Purpose: Entry point into the dungeon. Sets the mood with damp air and faint echoes.

2. **Mossy Chamber**
   - Exits: West to *Cave Entrance*, East to *Stagnant Pool*, South to *Ritual Room*
   - Purpose: Central hub of the dungeon. Overgrown with moss, suggestive of neglect and age. Serves as a decision point for players.

3. **Stagnant Pool**
   - Exits: West to *Mossy Chamber*, South to *Hidden Nook*
   - Purpose: A dead-end room with a murky pool. Players might find minor loot or clues here. Visually intriguing with potential for hidden dangers.

4. **Hidden Nook**
   - Exits: North to *Stagnant Pool*
   - Purpose: A secret area accessible through a narrow passage. Rewards exploration with hidden treasure or lore items.

5. **Ritual Room**
   - Exits: North to *Mossy Chamber*, South to *Ancient Vault*
   - Purpose: An eerie space with remnants of old rituals. Midpoint of the dungeon, where narrative elements unfold.

6. **Ancient Vault**
   - Exits: North to *Ritual Room*
   - Purpose: The final destination with the main treasure or boss encounter. A heavily secured chamber, hinting at its importance.

### Design Notes on Flow and Purpose

- **Flow**: The layout encourages exploration by branching out from the *Mossy Chamber*. Each path offers different narrative elements and potential rewards, creating an engaging journey through the dungeon.
- **Central Hub**: The *Mossy Chamber* acts as a central node, allowing players to choose their path. This design supports multiple play styles, whether players seek combat, story, or treasure.
- **Discovery**: The *Hidden Nook* is a reward for players who investigate thoroughly. It adds depth to the dungeon by offering a bonus area off the main path.
- **Pacing**: The dungeon starts with straightforward navigation but introduces branching paths and hidden areas to increase complexity as players progress.
- **Realism**: The layout reflects a natural cave system, enhanced by the presence of the *Ritual Room* and *Ancient Vault*, suggesting a forgotten history and purpose.
- **Narrative**: Storytelling is integrated through room design, with each area contributing to the dungeon's backstory, from the forgotten rituals to the ancient treasures.
